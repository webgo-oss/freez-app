Ecosystem










polar bear

The polar bear (Ursus maritimus) is a large Arctic carnivore with thick white fur. It hunts seals on sea ice and is an excellent swimmer. Climate change threatens its habitat.










penguin

The penguin is a flightless bird with a black-and-white body, living mostly in the Southern Hemisphere. It swims expertly and feeds on fish.










whale

Whales in the North include species like the bowhead whale, narwhal, and beluga. They have thick blubber for warmth and migrate through Arctic waters, feeding on fish and plankton.










seal

Seals are marine mammals found in cold waters. They have flippers for swimming, thick blubber for warmth, and hunt fish and squid. Many live in the Arctic and Antarctic regions.














Svalbard, Norway

A remote Arctic archipelago known for its glaciers, polar bears, and the magical Northern Lights. It offers boat tours, ice cave expeditions, and a glimpse of Arctic wildlife.

Quttinirpaaq National Park, Canada

A frozen wilderness with towering glaciers and rare Arctic animals like muskoxen. Ideal for extreme adventurers and nature lovers

Greenland

Home to massive icebergs, deep fjords, and Inuit culture. Visitors can enjoy dog sledding, whale watching, and breathtaking views of the Arctic landscape.

Franz Josef Land, Russia

A remote group of icy islands where polar bears roam. Accessible by icebreaker cruise, it offers an untouched Arctic experience.
